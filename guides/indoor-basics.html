Indoor plant care basics

Most people don't fail at plant parenting from a missing green thumb — they miss a few fundamentals.

1. Get the light right

Place houseplants where soft daylight falls. Bright indirect light suits most tropical indoor plants.

2. Water with patience

Check the top 1–2 inches of soil with your finger. Water when dry, then let excess drain away.

3. Choose breathable soil

Use a loose mix with cocopeat, compost, and perlite. Roots need air as much as moisture.

4. Feed lightly

During spring and summer, diluted fertilizer once a month is enough for most indoor plants.

5. Read the signals

  • Yellow leaves often mean too much water.
  • Drooping may mean thirst — or waterlogged roots.
  • Brown tips can point to dry air or mineral-heavy tap water.
